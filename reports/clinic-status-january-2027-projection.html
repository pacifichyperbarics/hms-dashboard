  • Open Oceanside correctly.

  • Move the Laguna phone system to Monterey.
  • Hire and activate Laguna liaison capacity.
  • Freeze Riverside one-chamber scope and budget.

  • Ramp Oceanside referrals and treatments.
  • Install Chula and Salinas compressor upgrades.
  • Enforce weekly clinic reporting.
  • Complete Riverside field work and equipment installation.
  • Standardize billing and authorizations across clinics.

December 2026-January 2027

  • Move Oceanside toward mature volume.
  • Open or launch Riverside in controlled ramp.
  • Validate Laguna and Monterey growth.
  • Confirm portfolio cash reserve and next capex cycle.
  • Make Chula capacity-expansion decision.

What could prevent this result

Equipment delaysCompressor, oxygen-generation or chamber-system delays could hold back Madras, Oceanside or Riverside.
Payer and authorization lagVolume can exist without cash if authorizations, documentation and claims are not closed promptly.
Incomplete weekly visibilityThe projection assumes every clinic reports treatments, authorizations, collections and blockers consistently.
Projected January 2027 position: HMS has four green clinics, two green/yellow clinics and one yellow ramp-up clinic. The portfolio is no longer dependent only on Chula, Salinas and Madras for operating momentum; Oceanside is a real contributor, Laguna and Monterey are orderly growth clinics, and Riverside is entering production under a one-chamber model.
